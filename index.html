Behind the Lens

Ten years on the trail, one frame at a time

What started as weekend hikes with a borrowed camera turned into a decade spent tracking migrations, waiting out weather in hides, and learning to read animal behavior well enough to anticipate a single moment.

The work isn't about the rarest sighting — it's about slowing down enough to see what's already there.

"You don't take the photograph. You wait long enough that it's offered to you."
